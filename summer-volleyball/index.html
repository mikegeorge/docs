Nutrition

At 5'5" and 93 lbs while growing and training hard, the single most important goal is eating enough. Under-fueling stalls strength gains, blunts growth, raises injury risk, and causes fatigue. Nothing in this plan works without adequate food.

Daily Framework

  • Three meals + 2–3 snacks. No skipped meals, especially breakfast on team days.
  • Protein at every meal — roughly 65–85 g/day total (eggs, chicken, beef, Greek yogurt, milk, beans).
  • Carbs are fuel — rice, pasta, potatoes, bread, fruit, oatmeal. Don't shortchange them, especially on team days.
  • Fats for growth and hormones: nuts, peanut butter, avocado, whole eggs, olive oil.

Around Workouts

  • 1–2 hrs before practice: real food — PB&J, oatmeal with fruit, turkey sandwich.
  • During 3-hr sessions: water throughout; a sports drink or fruit in the second half is reasonable in summer heat.
  • Within an hour after: protein + carbs. Chocolate milk is the classic; a smoothie or eating dinner soon after works too.

Micronutrients That Matter

  • Calcium + vitamin D — she's building peak bone density right now.
  • Iron — adolescent female athletes are the highest-risk group for deficiency. Red meat a couple times a week, beans, fortified cereal; pair plant sources with vitamin C. Persistent fatigue despite good sleep = ask the pediatrician about ferritin.
  • Supplements: generally unnecessary at 14. Skip pre-workouts entirely; any supplement question belongs in a pediatrician conversation.

What to Watch For

If appetite doesn't keep up with training — persistent fatigue, stalled progress, frequent minor injuries, or irregular/missed periods — those are signs she isn't fueling enough for her output. Worth a conversation with her pediatrician, ideally one familiar with athletes.

Recovery & Performance Practices

Sleep — the Biggest Lever

9–10 hours nightly. It beats every supplement and most training tweaks. Summer makes this easy to protect — protect it.

Hydration

In Louisiana heat, start hydrating the day before team sessions. Pale-yellow urine is the check. Electrolytes matter for 3-hour hot sessions.

Growth-Plate Awareness

She's at the classic age for Osgood-Schlatter (pain below the kneecap) and Sever's disease (heel pain) in jumping athletes. Neither is dangerous, but both mean cutting jump volume until pain resolves. Pain at a joint or bone during activity = stop and modify. Muscle soreness a day later = normal.

Ankle Prep

Volleyball's #1 injury. Single-leg balance (30 sec/side while brushing teeth) plus Monday's line hops go a long way.

Setter IQ

Watch high-level matches with attention on the setter — delivery choices against different blocks, tempo, disguise. Ten focused minutes beats an hour of background watching.

Tactical breakdown of two elite setters

Season Transition

Two weeks before tryouts: cut Saturday to 2 sets per exercise and drop Monday agility — arrive fresh, not fatigued. In-season: one maintenance strength session a week (2 sets per lift) preserves summer gains.
