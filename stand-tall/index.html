Strength Program

A posture-first plan built around two fixes — lengthen the tight calves and hip flexors, strengthen the glutes, hamstrings, shins, and core — while building real strength and muscle on top.

The Plan

Two Problems, One Strategy

Toe-walking and anterior pelvic tilt are mirror images of the same pattern: some muscles are tight and doing too much, others are weak and doing too little. Every session hits both sides.

ProblemTight — stretch itWeak — strengthen it
Toe-walkingCalves (gastrocnemius + soleus), AchillesTibialis anterior (shins), ankle control
Anterior pelvic tiltHip flexors, lower-back erectorsGlutes, hamstrings, abs
Every Day · ~8 min

Daily Mobility Routine

The highest-leverage piece of the whole program. Do it every day, even rest days — tissue length changes come from daily consistency, not workout intensity.

Toe-Walk Fix

Wall Calf Stretch — Straight & Bent Knee

2×45s each / leg

Straight knee hits the gastrocnemius; bent knee hits the soleus — the one most toe-walkers miss. Heel stays glued to the floor. Do both versions, both legs.

Heel Walking

3×20 steps

Toes pulled up hard the whole time, tall posture. This directly trains the opposite pattern of toe-walking and wakes up the shin muscles.

Before Each Session · ~5 min

Workout Warm-Up

Ankle rocks (above), then these three activation drills, then 15 arm circles and towel pull-aparts. The goal is to switch on the glutes and teach the ribs-down, neutral-pelvis position before loading it.

Glute Bridge

1×15 · 2s hold

Squeeze the glutes to lift, not the lower back. Ribs down. Two-second squeeze at the top of every rep.

Dead Bug

1×8 / side

Lower back pressed flat into the floor the entire time — that flat-back position is exactly the anti-pelvic-tilt pattern he needs. Slow and controlled.

Bird Dog

1×8 / side

Slow, square hips, no back arch as the leg extends. Imagine balancing a cup of water on the lower back.

Day 01

Lower Body — Squat Focus

Rest 60–90 seconds between sets (up to 2 minutes on goblet squats). Bodyweight versions first — add the kettlebell only when 3×12 is clean.

Squat → Goblet Squat

3×8–12

Heels flat the whole rep — this doubles as his ankle-dorsiflexion training. Start bodyweight; add a light KB at the chest once 3×12 is easy and clean.

Pelvic-Tilt Fix

Single-Leg Glute Bridge → KB Hip Thrust

3×10–12 / side

Hard glute squeeze at the top, ribs down — don't hyperextend the lower back to get higher. When single-leg reps are easy, move to two-leg hip thrusts with a KB on the hips.

3×4–6

On the Nordic bench: lower as slowly as possible (aim 5+ seconds), catch with hands, push back up with arms. Negatives only for months — full Nordics come much later, and that's normal.

Day 02

Upper Body + Core

Push, pull, press, carry. Every exercise here is also a posture drill in disguise — ribs stacked over pelvis, no lower-back arch.

Push-Up Progression

3×6–12

Incline → floor → deficit. Body in one straight line — sagging hips is the pelvic tilt sneaking in. If floor push-ups break down, raise the hands on a bench.

Watch on YouTube ↗

Single-Arm DB Row

3×8–12 / side

Hand and knee on the bench, flat back, pull the dumbbell to the hip. No twisting or yanking — control both directions.

Pelvic-Tilt Fix

Half-Kneeling DB Overhead Press

3×8–10 / side

Half-kneeling forces the back-leg glute on and blocks the lower-back arch most people press with. Ribs down, squeeze the down-leg glute, press straight up.

Pallof Press (Band) or Side Plank

3×10 / side

Anti-rotation core: press the band out and refuse to twist. No band? Side planks, 3×20–30 seconds per side.

3×30–40 steps

Day 03

Lower Body — Hinge Focus

The posterior-chain day: hamstrings and glutes do the heavy lifting. Master the RDL hinge before anything explosive. Finish with the wall calf stretches from the daily routine.

KB Romanian Deadlift

3×8–12

Hips back, flat back, soft knees, KB grazing the legs. Feel the hamstring stretch, then stand up by squeezing the glutes. The cornerstone lift of the whole program — start light.

Pelvic-Tilt Fix

Rear-Foot-Elevated Split Squat

3×8–10 / side

Bodyweight first, then hold dumbbells. Bonus: the back leg's hip flexor gets stretched while the front leg works — two birds, one exercise.

Nordic Curl Negatives

3×4–6

Same as Day 1. Progress by lowering slower before adding reps. Slow negatives are still one of the best hamstring builders on the planet.

3×12–15

Only after the RDL hinge is solid. It's a hip snap, not a squat and not an arm lift — glutes fire the bell forward, arms just steer.

How to Progress

Progression & Recovery

Progression Rules

  1. Weeks 1–4: bodyweight versions, mastering form. Add reps within the range before adding anything else. Run week one fully bodyweight — even where a kettlebell is listed.
  2. Week 5+: when he hits the top of a rep range on all sets with clean form two sessions in a row, add the smallest KB/DB jump (or the harder variation) and drop back to the bottom of the range.
  3. Nordics: slower negatives → more reps → less hand assistance → band-assisted full reps. A full unassisted Nordic may take 6–12 months. That's on schedule, not behind.
  4. Deload: every 6–8 weeks, take one easy week at half the sets.

Recovery & Habits

  • Sleep 8–10 hours. At 15, this is where the muscle actually gets built.
  • Protein at every meal — roughly a palm-sized portion or more.
  • Barefoot time at home builds foot and ankle awareness. Cue "heels first" when walking, but let the strength work do most of the talking.
  • Re-check at 8 weeks: deeper flat-heel squat, easier heel walking, and less lower-back arch when standing relaxed are the wins to look for.
